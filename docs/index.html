Five largest protests in U.S. History

By Austin Cope

Freedom of assembly is enshrined in the U.S. constitution, and Americans have increasingly exercised that right. Four of five largest protests in U.S. history, by estimated number of attendees, have occurred since 2017.

  1. George Floyd Racial Justice Demostrations, 15-26 Million Attendees Nationwide, 2020
  2. Earth Day, 20 Million Attendees Nationwide, 1970
  3. 2017 Women's March, 3.3-5.6 Million Attendees Nationwide, 2017
  4. March for Our Lives, 1.2-2 Million Attendees Nationwide, 2018
  5. 2018 Women's March, 1.5 Million Attendees Nationwide, 2018

Source: Wikipedia, List of protests and demonstrations in the United States by size
